Chapter 17 — Soren  
  
The OA offices were quiet in the hour before noon. She had come in early, filed two reports, was working through the eastern district logistics when Soren appeared with two cups and set one on the corner of her desk without asking.  
Four years of this.  
He took the secondary chair. His reports were current — she had seen them in the shared system before she came in. Another face flagged from the north quarter surveillance; the third man from the inciting attack placed now in two subsequent incidents. He had made clean progress while she had been elsewhere.  
He wrapped both hands around his cup. “You ran a records query at the Institute three days ago.”  
Not quite looking at her. The mode she knew: professional observation, awaiting professional response.  
“Bioscience division,” she said. “A twelve-year-old sealed collection under Harlan’s administrative hold. I’ve been working the researcher thread since spring — I wanted to understand what she’d been doing before she left.”  
“And?”  
“Methodological dead end. Research files. Nothing operational.”  
He turned his cup once — a small rotation, clockwise, a habit she had catalogued without meaning to over four years. “There’s nothing in your log from the three hours after.”  
“I walked.”  
A pause. He looked at her, which he’d been mostly not doing. She held it.  
“All right,” he said. “Anything I should have from you for the formal request on the third man?”  
“Let me know when you’re ready to move. I’ll have what you need.”  
He stood. Picked up his cup. There was a moment — not long — when he simply stood at her desk and didn’t say anything. Not searching. Not deciding. The quality of someone who has read something and chosen not to read it aloud.  
Then: “The Council summary. End of week.”  
“Yes.”  
He left.  
—————  
She stayed at the desk and looked at the logistics file she had been working before he came in.  
*He saw it*, she thought. *He has been seeing it for months.*  
*He saw it*, she thought. *He has been seeing it for months.*  
He was not someone who missed things. Eleven years in the OA, the same as her, and he had run the investigation well — the supply chain thread, the faces, the coordinated operational signatures across three facilities. He had done the work that came from applying good method to tractable problems and not sitting inside the ones that weren’t. She had watched him do this for four years: the surface efficiency, the forward motion, the case-shaped questions and case-shaped answers. The knowledge that what fell outside the case belonged outside the case.  
She had been that person. She knew the register exactly.  
She had filed a report three years ago that said *expected sensory adjustment period *and gone home and not slept and told herself it was fine. She had made decisions in the last four months she hadn’t logged and hadn’t explained to herself, only understood afterward that they had been made. She could not locate the point where she had stopped being the version of herself who did what Soren did — clearly and without residue — and had become whatever she was now. There was no single point. There had been too many exits, each one small, that she had taken without naming them as exits.  
He had been giving her those, too. The unlogged eastern district afternoon. The canal contact she’d offered as explanation. And now this — three hours of walking he had no way to verify, a redirect he had no operational reason to accept, and he had said *all right* and let her keep what she was carrying.  
He had been giving her those, too. The unlogged eastern district afternoon. The canal contact she’d offered as explanation. And now this — three hours of walking he had no way to verify, a redirect he had no operational reason to accept, and he had said *all right* and let her keep what she was carrying.  
That was not a small thing. She had not thanked him for it and would not.  
Outside, the rain worked its way down the window. A rat crossed the drainage channel in the street below and was gone before she had looked up.  
She turned back to the logistics file. What had crossed the drainage channel was already gone. The work remained.
