The Unaccounted

It had no field for the hand.
For the hand that does not release
the hand beside it. For the grip
used as an anchor in the dark.
For the leaving that doesn't happen — the slip
that remains a choice not made.
For the body that stays past use,
past plan, past the last point
where staying has something to lose.

For presence with no outcome.
For the only act remaining
chosen because it is the only thing.
Not rescue. Not reclaiming
what is gone. Just this:
the room, the hand, the breathing.
One person beside one person in the dark.
The one thing the data has no field for.

The darkness holds.
The data runs.
The system does exactly what it was built to do.

And inside it: this.
Two people. One hand.

Not recorded.
Not designed for.

The experiment was built
to study how we end.

It did not end here.
