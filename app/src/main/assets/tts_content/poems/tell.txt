*Tell*


I try to make myself 
the size of a thing not worth naming. 
A paperclip. A crumb. 
The breath held between questions.

But the mirror in the room remembers. 
The lipstick remembers. The hem. 
The careful hour 
I thought was mine.

My face goes hot the way it does 
when a thing you kept in a drawer 
is suddenly on the table 
and everyone is quiet.

The room already knows my name. 
The room has always known. 
I just didn't know 
the room was listening.


And now I live on the other side 
of a door that will not close. 
The woman in the mirror 
is not a secret anymore. 
She was never a secret. She was only mine.
