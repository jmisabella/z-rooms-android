*I Used to Say Hello*


Recurring feeling that I recognize somebody,
that I know the face —
sometimes I think there are only
twenty, maybe thirty faces
I have ever seen,
repeated in different arrangements
across strangers.

I used to say hello.
I'd be told I was mistaken.
I'd apologize, walk on,
and a month later see her again
wearing a different name,
different hair, a different life
she was perfectly sure was hers.

This foolish jester
who sees the arrangement —
she has learned
not to say so.
The asking is its own admission
and I am not ready
to hear the answer out loud.

So I nod the small nod
you give to anyone.
I let the woman in the checkout line
be a stranger, officially.
I move my cart along

and keep to myself
the small insistent knowledge
that I have met her before,
that I will meet her again,
that the world is smaller
than it is supposed to be
and I am the only one
who seems to notice.
