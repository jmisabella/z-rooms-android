*Holding*

All day I have been a cup
held very still.
Nothing in me has spilled
and nothing has been poured out,
and this is what they call
managing.
 The jaw remembers first.
Then the shoulders. Then
the place behind the sternum
where I've been keeping
the small animal
of the day.

I sit on the edge of the bed
and let the hands open.
They do not know
what to do
with nothing to carry.


The body asks,
is it over.
The body asks again.
The body does not believe me
when I say yes.

Somewhere a kettle.
Somewhere a small warmth
that used to find the foot of the bed.
Somewhere a version of me
who did not spend the day
being careful.

I tell the body:
*you can put it down.*
*you can put it down.*
*you can put it down.*

It takes the whole evening
to believe me.
