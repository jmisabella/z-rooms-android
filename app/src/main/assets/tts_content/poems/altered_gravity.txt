*Altered Gravity*


We changed the gravity.
Could almost float.
Falling from any height
was a soft glide, a scrape,
bouncing up again
slower each time.

The only task for the day
was to arrest the descent.
To stay.
We told ourselves
we could live here —
in the bliss of arrested fall.

But gravity always came back.
Iron in the ankles.
The ground enlarging fast.
*Is this what it's like to be normal?*
*Is normal this heavy?*


You were running from something.
I was trying to keep up.
When you got away
I was left chasing
a field past the horizon —

and did not notice, for years,
the walls I had built
around myself while running.
Cold stone. Completely dark.
Weighted to the ground.

It took a long time
to see I was also the warden.
That the lock
was there to be picked.
That it had always
been free to try.
