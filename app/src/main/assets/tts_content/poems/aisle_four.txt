*Aisle Four*


I used to hide behind a mask
to fit in with the crowd,
so strangers, they would pay no mind
at anything at all.

Wore the same outfit everyday —
bulky and clunky and ill-fitted.
A uniform of quiet no,
a self I half-admitted.

Pretended to embrace that mask.
while something in me slept.
Enfolded in that outer shell
that the strangers would accept.

I hated wearing that ugly face,
that flat rehearsed disguise —
the stranger in the mirror who
wore my mother’s eyes.


I couldn’t go on anymore
carrying that shell —
carrying that empty weight
the world was keen to sell. 
I pulled on colors, softer lines,
the shape that felt like mine —
a quiet grace I hadn’t known,
a stillness half-divine.

Now the strangers
their eyes like broken glass —
I push my cart down aisle four
and let the daggers pass

The mask is gone. The shell is cracked.
The aisles are bright and long.
I carry what is mine to carry.
I have been here all along.
