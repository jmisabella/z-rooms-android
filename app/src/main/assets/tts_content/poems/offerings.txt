*Offerings*


I stack my small offerings on the sill — 
the careful word, the hour given freely, 
the work done right when no one was looking. 
Rain takes them. Or wind. Or nothing at all.

I have learned the shape of a room 
that does not turn when I enter, 
the particular quiet of a name 
spoken into a phone that keeps ringing.

Still the hands keep doing their work. 
Still the mouth keeps shaping its please 
and its thank you and its I understand, 
and the sky keeps its indifferent grey.

Sometimes in the quiet a voice returns —
not hers, not anyone's, but mimicking the shape —
*Volition is gone. The chance has but passed.*
*What remains is yours to carry.*


And when evening arrives, 
the lamp I turn on, it’s only for me, 
the cat not here anymore 
the house quiet.

A quiet not punishment, 
I keep telling myself. Quiet’s not the verdict. 
It’s only the sound a room makes 
when you have finally stopped asking it to answer.
