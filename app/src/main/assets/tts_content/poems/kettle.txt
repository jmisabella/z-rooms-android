*Kettle*


Some mornings the kettle
catches me before I have arranged
the face I wear to meet the day.

I am reaching past it
for the cup, for the spoon,
for the ordinary apparatus,

and there in the curve of it —
warped, half-lit, not quite mine
but close enough —

someone I recognize
without the usual lag,
the usual small adjustment

the mirror asks of me.
No one is watching.
The kettle is not watching.


It is only doing
what a kettle does,
which is hold heat, hold shape,

return the room to itself
in a softer outline.
And for the length of a breath

I do not have to do anything
with what I saw.
I do not have to make it mean.

The kettle clicks off.
The reflection goes
back to being steam, and light,

and the plain fact
of a morning
that did not require me

to explain myself
to anyone,
not even to her.
