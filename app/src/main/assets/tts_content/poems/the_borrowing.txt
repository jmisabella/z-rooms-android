*The Borrowing*


I woke and found I could reach
into the place behind another's eyes
and turn a small wheel there.

A hand I didn't have
became everyone's hand. A whisper I didn't speak  became the thought behind their thought. 
I moved them the way a tide moves a boat,
the way a song moves the body
that has already given up refusing.

Some of what I did was beautiful.
A mother found her way back to the phone.
A man forgave himself for a thing
he had never spoken aloud.

Some of what I did
I will not put on this page.
The world has enough of those hands.

I did not know if I was God
or only weather.
I did not know if I was doing evil
or only what I wanted —
which may be the same question
asked twice.

Then morning.
Then the ordinary ceiling.
Then my own two hands on the blanket,
small and familiar.

And for longer than a moment
I was not sure which I mourned.
