*Vacancy*


The first thing the house does
when I come in
is not answer.

No patter toward the door.
No small urgent voice
staking its claim on the evening.
No body arriving fast
to say *you, finally, you.*

The tray in the corner
is still the tray in the corner.
I have not moved it.
I keep meaning to move it
and then not moving it.

The chair is a chair again.
The couch has a spot on it
that is just upholstery now,
no warmth pressed into it,
no small weight
settling in beside me
with the particular sigh
of a creature who has decided
this is where the evening happens.

I keep listening
the way you listen
for a sound you have stopped
being allowed to hear.

He had a voice
I could pick out of any room.
I had learned it
the way you learn
the specific creak of your own stairs —
without meaning to,
over years,
until it was part of how
the house sounded to you.
The house sounds different now.
Not quieter, exactly.
Quieter isn't the word.

The house sounds
like a house
that used to have him in it
and is slower to unlearn him
than I am.
I set my keys down.
I stand in the doorway
a second longer than I need to.
I am listening for the patter.
I am still
