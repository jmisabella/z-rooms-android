*Brightest Day*


It arrives on the brightest day,
uninvited, already at work
before I notice the light has changed.

The yard is still the yard.
The grass still doing
what grass does in April.

But the green has gone
one step back from itself,
the way a voice does
on a bad line —

still the voice,
still saying the words,
just no longer quite
reaching the room.


A stillness takes hold.
The day I was going to have
is somewhere on the other side
of a pane I cannot find the edge of.

The chill finds the soil first.
Then me.
The sky keeps its blue
the way a kettle keeps its shape —

doing what it does,
indifferent,
holding heat
for no one in particular.

An everlasting grey
that is not the weather.
That was never the weather.
