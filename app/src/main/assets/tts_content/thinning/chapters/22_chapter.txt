_Chapter 22_

She had not been down to the greenhouses since the planting-out, and she was not going to go down, though she had not put it to herself as a thing she had decided so much as found it already decided when the days came up and held and went and she was not in them. There was the thing her sister had said at the last, over the bed, about not bringing it that near to her again — she had it the way she had the other thing said the once down the row of the tomatoes, not as words she went over but as a thing that was in the not-going now, down under it, the way the cold was down under the floor before it came up; and there was the not-going itself, which had been in her before the thing was said and would have been in her without it. The one had come and found the other already there.

The rooms were dark and she was up in them. The lamp stood on the shelf with the oil in it and she had not crossed to it; there was nothing the dark kept her from that she had been going to do. The account was squared on the table where the day had left it and the notebook closed beside it on the sessions got down and not carried over — the weddings still, and the babies on the part-sheet with nothing under the three lines, and the funerals behind them — and they sat on the table the same as the cups did, the two of them with the rings dried in the bottoms that she had not moved, things she had the place of and went past. She moved among them in the dark without taking any of them up, not going around them so much as through the middle of where they were, the way you cross a room you have the dark of by heart; and the box was out at the white house with the lid off it for a next time, and the dates ran on clean further into the notebook with nothing under them, and her sister was down the other way where the land dropped, at the work in the made light; and she had each of them where it was and put a hand to none of it.

The planting-out came up once while she stood there, the way the last days came up now, not gone toward, only there a moment and then not — the long bare bed taking the young in down its length, and the real light back along the house, and her own hand open over the bed in the warm of the day; and her sister's hand come up out of the soil and over to it, the warm of it crossing whole into her open hand, and the hand with it, laid into it a moment over the young, and then down again to the work. It came up and was there and she did not hold it, and her attention went off it onto the black of the window, and it was not there anymore.

The window over the table held its black and gave back no street and no room, only the dark of the night against the dark of the rooms behind her. She did not look at it the way she did not look at the things she did not look at. There was nothing out across the way to mark — the few that had still been lit when she last sat to it had gone dark long since, and she did not go looking along the night for what was left, and no count came up out of the dark to be kept, because the dark gave it nothing to start on. She stood in it. The cold came up into the rooms the way it came, from the corners and then the floor, and got to her, and she let it, the way she let the warm of a day go through her unmarked when there was a day; and the dark was the one she was easiest in now, easier than the going light and easier by far than the held bright middle that was the one that found her out, so that being up at this hour was not a thing she came to. It was where she was.

The night ran on toward the far end of itself, where the cold would begin at last to go and the dark begin to lift off the window, and the day come up over the valley after it and hold at the angle it held, hours of the held light, good light to walk down to the bed in and to do the day's work by; and she would not be down at the head of it, and the young would go on in the ground the same without her there. The lamp stayed on the shelf where it was. The account stayed squared on the table with the work in the notebook a hand's breadth from it and not carried the hand's breadth across. She had not been down since the planting-out. She was not going down.
