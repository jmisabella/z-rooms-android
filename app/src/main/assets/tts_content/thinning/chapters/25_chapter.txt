_Chapter 25_

She was up in the day, which she was not, most days now, and the light was the held light of the long middle of it, the flat bright that came across the rooms and lay where it lay and did not move, the same of it on the boards it had been on when she found herself in it, hours of it yet to hold before it would go all at once. It came the whole way in, into the rooms that had the dark of the nights in them the most of the hours she was in them, and it found her out the way it did now, the going light easier on her than it and the dark easier by far than the going light, so that being up in it was a thing she came to and not where she was. The warm the day had in it had got into her where she stood, the face and the hands, and she had let it go through her unmarked the way she let it; and what was left her to keep of the day was the cold that stood off yet in the back of the rooms where the held light did not reach, in the one corner it had never got to and along the far wall, the night's cold not gone out of them.

The things were where they were and the light was on them. The account squared to the edge of the table in the very light it was meant to be done in, the long flat bright the small hand was to be read by and carried over by, the light there had been none of at the table the nights she sat to it; the first pen across the empty sheets; the cups with the rings dried in them that she had not moved. The light lay over the whole of it and gave her each thing of it, and she had the place of each the way she had it in the dark, and she put her hand to none of it; only it was not the dark she went around them in now but the light, the light setting each of them out for her to go around, the account in the work-light not worked, the day full on the table that was a daytime table for the day's work, with the day on it and the work not done. Out past the window, down the way the land dropped, the houses ran along the street the empty among the few with someone in them yet, and she had the count of those going, the kept among the gone, a pane somebody still washed, a curtain hung in a low room; it came to nothing and ran on under her, neither lost nor kept nor stopped, a thing the part of her that did it did while the rest of her stood in the light, and she let it run.

The walk went down that way in this light, the down-and-around one; it had always been a daytime thing, the walk, you went in the held bright and came back in the going light, and she had not gone down it since the planting-out, since the bed was filled, and would not. Down at the end of it her sister was at the work in the light come back, in the houses where the land dropped, the young she had set into the cleared bed grown on in the ground by now and the year up out of the bottom of itself; she was there, at it, the same, doing the all of it the way she did it, and Claire knew her there the way she knew the cups were on the table.

Her attention went down the way to her sister the way it went out toward a thing, and what was down there did not come. The soil dried along the side of the jaw where a hand had pushed the hair and not known it; the hands at the work, the knuckles standing, no waste in them since Claire was a child; the square set of her to a thing she had long ago decided what she would do about; the level voice with nothing breaking in it that had said no down the row of the tomatoes and no over the bed — none of it came up the way to her, the way it had stopped coming this long while, and what came instead, nearer, the rest of the way in, was the other, the one that came when she put her mind down the way to her sister now, whose hand came over and turned and laid itself the whole of the way into hers, the having of it near and easy and the soil not on it. It came up this once far enough to be seen before it went, the way a thing is seen in the held light that the dark had been keeping off her: that the one down the way at the work, whole, the same, was not the one that came; that the one that came was the one she had of her sister now, and the one down the way she had the place of and could not get. It came up to be seen and it did not do to her the thing it should have done, did not go through her the way the cold went or open under her the way a hurt opens; it was the thing it was and she saw it and that was the whole of what it did, of course it was, real or not real, which she had never known of any of it and did not know now. It was there a moment and then it was not held, and it went down under the rest of it the way the things went down, and she was in the light with the day on the table and the cold off in the back of the rooms.

The light held at its angle across the boards the while, not moving and not moving and then it would go all at once when the day got to where it got to over the valley; and it would go off the table and up the wall and out of the rooms, and the dark would come up after it the way it came, and she would be easier in it than she was in this, and the thing that had come up to be seen would be down under the rest again and not seen. There were hours of the held light yet before that, and she was in it.
