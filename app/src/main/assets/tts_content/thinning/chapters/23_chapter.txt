_Chapter 23_

The date stood ruled at the top of the page in the small clean way, and under it nothing, and the page before it ruled the same and nothing under it, and the one before that, the dates going down the clean pages of the notebook one after another with nothing put down for any of them. She had ruled them as they came. A date she had set for the next time would pass and she would not have gone, and she would rule the next one for the time after, the way she had always ruled the next date at the end of a session — only there was no session at the end of which to rule it now, so she ruled it at the table, on the day it should have been, and that one would pass the same, and she would rule the one behind it. The dates went on down the pages and the visits did not, and the gap between the last she had filled and the one she was ruling had got to its size without a day in it she could have set her hand on and said that was the one she had decided against.

It was a long while now since the planting-out. The day past the window came up earlier and held longer and took the cold out of the rooms sooner than it had, the year gone on up out of the bottom of itself; and the young her sister had set into the cleared bed would be on in it, grown past the size they went in at, and Claire had not been down to see them. But that was the one not-going, the one down where the land dropped, and it had been settled since the bed was filled. This was the other. This was the one up the way that went up and along, to the white house at the top of the street, the walk she had made of a morning and come back from in the going light, the streets between gone over to empty houses the whole way up, and the bright room at the end of it.

Ruth would be in the room. The box would be out on the table, square to the edge of it with the lid off, the ones she had not reached squared down inside; she set it out so at the end of a day, for the next time, and she had set it out the last time for a next time that was Claire. It had been standing out for her through all the dates that had gone down the clean pages, in the south light the wide windows took the best of, the way Ruth waited in the chair without saying she waited, having said the once that she was not going anywhere between now and whenever Claire was next by. A date ruled clean at the top of a page was a next time still coming, and a Thursday she had not walked had the next Thursday under it.

She had the notebook in her hand and the pen unclipped to rule the date, and her thumb was in the clean leaves of it going to the page to rule, the leaves passing under the thumb one and the next, and she had the count of them coming up the way it came now, up by the ones, arriving nowhere, because she did not know how many she had let go past the thumb or how many were left to the back of the book. The page came up clean under the thumb to take the date. She did not rule it. The hand that had ruled the date at the end of every afternoon in the bright room, and on a hundred evenings at the dark table for an afternoon there had not been, did not go down to the page; and she let the count of the leaves go out of her, and closed the notebook on the clean page and on the count both, and set it down on the table by the account.

It was not a thing she put to herself. She had stopped saying she would come the next time, or the one after, a long way back and in another room, without having sat down to it and decided; and the ruling of the date had gone on after the saying of it had stopped, the last of the thing to keep on, the hand doing it when the rest of her had quit. Now the hand had quit it too.

The things in the rooms with her stood where they stood. The box out up there in the light for the next time; the clean pages running on past the last ruled date to the back of the book with no date among them; her sister down the other way, at the work. She had the place of each of them and did not look toward it, and nothing in her went out toward it, there at the table in the dark with the notebook shut and set by.

Up the other way the box stood out in the dark of the room now, the light long off the table, square to the edge with the lid off, the ones Ruth had not reached squared down in it. The dark would lift off the wide windows in the morning and the south light come back along the table to it, and it would be out in the light the same, for the next time. There was no next time ruled for it now, and the morning would come the same, and the one after.
